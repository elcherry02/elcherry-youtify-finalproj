BLACKPINK


BLACKPINK, is a South-Korean Girl Group formed by YG Entertainment

consists of members Jisoo, Jennie, Rose and Lisa. The group debuted their

number one entry on the South Korean Gaon Digital Chart and

World Digital Song Sales Charts in August 2016 with a single album,

Square One, including "Whistle" and "Boombayah."

BILLIE EILISH


Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell is an American singer-songwriter.

First came to the public's attention in 2015 when Darkroom, an imprint of

Interscope Records, her debut track, "Ocean Eyes." On December 18, 2001,

she was born in Los Angeles, California, United States. She is currently

20 years old and has published numerous songs on her own. Some of her

are the Happier than Ever (2021), When We All Fall Asleep (2019),

and Live at Third Man Records the same year.

DOJA CAT


Amala Ratna Zandile Dlamini, also known as Doja Cat, is an American rapper

and vocalist. She was born and raised in Los Angeles, where she started

writing and posting music as a teenager. Her songs are quite well-liked,

particularly on the TikTok app. Her tracks from her album Planet Her,

including Kiss Me More, Woman, Need to Know, and Get Into It (Yuh),

have been popular online.

AIR SUPPLY


Graham Russell, an English singer-songwriter and guitarist,

Russell Hitchcock,an Australian lead vocalist, make up the soft rock

duo Air Supply. They enjoyed a string of successes all over the world,

including eight top ten hits in the US in the early 1980s.

They started out in 1975 with a variety of backing vocalists and

musicians. On December 1, 2013, at the yearly ARIA Awards, the

Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) admitted

Air Supply into their Hall of Fame.

DJ SNAKE


William Sami Étienne Grigahcine, better known as DJ Snake, is a French DJ

and record producer from Paris who was born on June 13, 1986.

A Grammy-nominated producer and musician, DJ Snake made his breakthrough

on the global music scene in 2013 with the tracks "Bird Machine" and

"Turn Down for What."

THE CARPENTERS


Siblings Karen and Richard Carpenter formed the American vocal and

instrumental group The Carpenters. Together, Karen's contralto

vocals and Richard's abilities in harmonizing, arranging, and

composing created a distinctive soft musical style. The Carpenters released

10 albums, countless singles, and numerous television specials

during the course of their 14-year career.

ARTHUR NERY


Born and raised in Cagayan De Oro, Arthur Nery was always into music

Fsince he was young. In fact, music actually runs in the family as his

parents are singers. He credits them for inspiring him to make music.

“When I was younger, we used to sing like worship songs before sleeping.

So ‘yun siguro, doon na-open ‘yung mind ko sa music and dun na

nagtuloy-tuloy.” Aside from this, Arthur’s dad was also into jazz and

blues, so hearing artists like Frank Sinatra and Michael Buble wasn’t an

uncommon at the Nery household. But while Arthur did dabble in making

and writing music and songs while in high school and college, he mostly

kept it to himself or close friends and family.
